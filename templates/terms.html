01 Scoring Methodology & Proprietary Technology

Lens League Apex uses the Apex DDI Engine, a proprietary AI-powered scoring system evaluating photographs across five modules: Degree of Difficulty (DoD), Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment (DM), Wonder Factor, and Affective Quotient (AQ).

Each module is weighted according to the genre submitted. Final scores are calculated by a weighted formula subject to Apex Layer rules including Soul Bonus, Humanity Check, Iconic Wall, and Plateau Penalty adjustments. Specific formula weights are proprietary and not disclosed in full.

Important: Scores reflect the engine's assessment at the time of submission. Calibration updates may affect future images but will not retroactively alter existing scores.
Proprietary Technology Notice: The Apex DDI Engine — including its module structure (DoD, Disruption, DM, Wonder, AQ), genre-calibrated weight tables, tier boundaries, archetype taxonomy, Soul Bonus criteria, Apex Byline framework, calibration logs, REF example library, and all associated scoring logic and prompts — constitutes original proprietary intellectual property exclusively owned by Lens League Apex. Reverse engineering, imitation, reproduction, or use of any element of this methodology as the basis of a competing scoring or evaluation system is strictly prohibited and may constitute a criminal offence under applicable IP law. Score outputs, module breakdowns, and calibration benchmarks may not be used to train, develop, or calibrate any competing photography rating product, commercial or otherwise. Lens League Apex reserves the right to pursue civil and criminal remedies against any violating party.

02 AI Limitations Disclaimer

The Apex DDI Engine is subject to the inherent limitations of artificial intelligence, including occasional misidentification of subject matter or technique, scoring variations due to model non-determinism, potential bias against styles underrepresented in training data, and inability to account for contextual information not visible in the image. Scores should be treated as one data point in a photographer's development, not a definitive judgment of artistic merit.

03 Three-Tier Judging Framework

All competitions operate under a three-tier judging framework combining AI objectivity, human perception, and legal defensibility.

Tier 1 — Apex DDI Engine

Every submitted image is automatically evaluated. This produces an objective DDI score and tier classification, establishing initial leaderboard position instantly on submission.

Tier 2 — Blind Peer-to-Peer Rating

Applicable to Monthly Contests, POTY, and Open Competition category ranking. Subscribers rate each other's images under fully blind conditions — rater identity, photographer identity, and DDI scores are all concealed. Each image receives a minimum of five independent peer ratings per monthly cycle. Participation is mandatory for competition progression beyond Tier 1. Subscribers have a monthly rating obligation of 20 images, fulfilled through the Rating Credits system.

Tier 3 — Jury

For Monthly Contests and POTY: triggered automatically when a peer rating diverges from the DDI score by more than 1.5 points, or when a formal dispute is raised. For the Open Competition: mandatory for Grand Open Winner selection — the jury evaluates all 8 category winners and selects the single Grand Open Winner. Jury composition rotates each cycle and is never disclosed. Jury decisions at Tier 3 are final and binding. A photographer may not dispute a Tier 3 decision. Raising a dispute costs one Rating Credit; the same image may not be disputed twice.

Score Visibility Policy: Numerical DDI scores are visible only to the individual photographer on their personal dashboard. All public-facing pages display tier classification only (Apprentice, Practitioner, Master, Grandmaster, Legend). This protects photographer privacy and the integrity of the scoring system.

04 Competitions, POTY, Open Competition & Prizes

Monthly Competitions

  • Determined by DDI (Tier 1) → Blind Peer Rating (Tier 2) → Jury override (Tier 3, where triggered).
  • Separate Camera and Mobile tracks. Genre-based within each track.
  • All scored images uploaded within the calendar month are automatically entered — no separate entry required.
  • Leaderboards display tier classification only. Monthly reset on the first of each calendar month.

Photographer of the Year (POTY)

  • Annual, per genre, across Camera and Mobile tracks independently. 16 competitions total: 8 genres × 2 tracks.
  • Eligibility: minimum 24 qualifying images in that genre during the subscription year (6 months × 4 images).
  • POTY score: average of the photographer's top 6 DDI scores in that genre across all qualifying images for the year.
  • A dynamic percentile cutoff is recalculated monthly as images accumulate, until the 12th month submission deadline closes.
  • Subscribers from Month 1 may have up to 48 images evaluated; Month 4, up to 32; Month 7, up to 24 (minimum). This proportional difference is a legitimate function of tenure, balanced by the top-6 methodology rewarding quality over volume.
  • Additional purchased images are included in POTY evaluation on equal terms.
  • Prize pool: ₹50,00,000. Camera Gold ₹1,50,000 / Silver ₹75,000 / Bronze ₹50,000. Mobile Gold ₹75,000 / Silver ₹50,000 / Bronze ₹25,000. Grand POTY Camera ₹10,00,000. Grand POTY Mobile ₹6,00,000. Full prize structure in the Contest Rules.
  • Judged: DDI → Blind Peer Rating → Jury (triggered).

Open Competition

  • Annual, paid entry. ₹50 per image per genre entry. No track split — Camera and Mobile compete in the same pool.
  • 8 genre categories: Wildlife, Street, Landscape, People, Wedding, Macro, Drone & Aerial, Creative.
  • One winner per genre. No Silver or Bronze.
  • Category prize: ₹75,000 per genre winner (8 × ₹75,000 = ₹6,00,000).
  • Grand Open Winner: selected by jury from the 8 category winners. Prize: ₹2,00,000 in addition to category prize (combined ₹2,75,000).
  • Total Open prize pool: ₹8,00,000.
  • Entry opens 3 months before Grand Prix. Images must have been previously scored on the platform under the submitting photographer's account.
  • Entry is deliberate and paid — not automatic. A photographer may enter multiple genres but only one image per genre.

General

  • Prize eligibility requires a verified account in good standing with no active policy violations.
  • Lens League Apex reserves the right to disqualify entries, withhold prizes, or void results in cases of fraud, duplicates, policy violations, or AI-generated content.
  • All prize decisions are final. Full rules, prize structures, and legal immunity clauses are in the separate Contest Rules & Legal Framework.

05 Peer Rating System & Anti-Gaming Policy

Lens League Apex operates a zero-tolerance policy against manipulation of the peer rating system. The following constitute violations subject to immediate account suspension:

Prohibited Conduct

  • Coordinated voting rings: Organising with other subscribers to exchange favourable ratings. The system tracks rating correlations and flags statistically improbable relationships for audit. Correlated raters are never assigned the same image pool.
  • Targeted sabotage: Systematically rating a specific photographer's work below norms. Outlier ratings deviating more than two standard deviations from peer consensus are excluded and trigger audit.
  • Low-effort rating: Submitting ratings in under 15 seconds per image. A minimum time-on-image threshold is enforced; sub-threshold ratings are invalid.
  • Multiple accounts: Creating secondary accounts to inflate ratings. New accounts are ineligible for peer rating for the first 30 days and until at least 3 scored images have been submitted.
  • Score inference: Systematically correlating ratings with competition outcomes to reverse-engineer DDI parameters. API rate limiting and score obfuscation are in place to detect and prevent this.
  • Jury gaming: Filing repeated disputes to force jury review. Each dispute costs one Rating Credit; the same image may not be disputed twice.

Rating Credits & Trusted Rater Status

Every image rated earns one Rating Credit, required to unlock peer evaluation of your own submissions. Subscribers who do not meet their monthly obligation of 20 ratings will have their images held in the peer queue until the obligation is met.

Subscribers whose ratings demonstrate consistent alignment with peer consensus over time earn Trusted Rater status, visible on their profile. Trusted Raters with Legend tier are eligible to apply for Jury Legend status, subject to Lens League Apex approval.
