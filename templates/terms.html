01 Scoring Methodology & Proprietary Technology

Lens League Apex uses the Apex DDI Engine, a proprietary AI-powered scoring system evaluating photographs across five modules: Degree of Difficulty (DoD), Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment (DM), Wonder Factor, and Affective Quotient (AQ). Each module is weighted according to the genre submitted. Final scores are calculated by a weighted formula subject to Apex Layer rules including Soul Bonus, Humanity Check, Iconic Wall, and Plateau Penalty adjustments.

Important: Scores reflect the engine's assessment at the time of submission. Calibration updates may affect future images but will not retroactively alter existing scores.
Proprietary Technology Notice: The Apex DDI Engine — including its module structure, genre-calibrated weight tables, tier boundaries, archetype taxonomy, Soul Bonus criteria, Apex Byline framework, calibration logs, REF example library, and all associated scoring logic and prompts — constitutes original proprietary intellectual property exclusively owned by Lens League Apex. Reverse engineering, imitation, reproduction, or use of any element of this methodology as the basis of a competing scoring or evaluation system is strictly prohibited and may constitute a criminal offence under applicable IP law. Score outputs, module breakdowns, and calibration benchmarks may not be used to train, develop, or calibrate any competing photography rating product. Lens League Apex reserves the right to pursue civil and criminal remedies against any violating party.

02 AI Limitations Disclaimer

The Apex DDI Engine is subject to the inherent limitations of artificial intelligence, including occasional misidentification of subject matter or technique, scoring variations due to model non-determinism, potential bias against styles underrepresented in training data, and inability to account for contextual information not visible in the image. Scores should be treated as one data point in a photographer's development, not a definitive judgment of artistic merit.

03 Three-Tier Judging Framework

All competitions operate under a three-tier judging framework combining AI objectivity, human perception, and legal defensibility.

Tier 1 — Apex DDI Engine

Every submitted image is automatically evaluated, producing an objective DDI score and tier classification.

Tier 2 — Blind Peer-to-Peer Rating

Applicable to Monthly Contests, POTY, and Open Competition category ranking only. Does not apply to Body of Work. Subscribers rate each other's images under fully blind conditions — rater identity, photographer identity, and DDI scores are all concealed. Each image receives a minimum of five independent peer ratings per monthly cycle.

Tier 3 — Jury

For Monthly Contests and POTY: triggered when peer rating diverges from DDI score by more than 1.5 points, or when a formal dispute is raised. For Body of Work: mandatory Jury Legends evaluation. For Open Competition: mandatory for Grand Open Winner selection. Jury composition is never disclosed. Jury decisions at Tier 3 are final and binding.

Score Visibility Policy: Numerical DDI scores are visible only to the individual photographer on their personal dashboard. All public-facing pages display tier classification only. This protects photographer privacy and the integrity of the scoring system.

05 Image Specifications & Technical Requirements

The following specifications apply universally to all images submitted for scoring and competition on Lens League Apex.

  • Format: Original JPEG only. Re-saved JPEGs, screenshots, scans, and images exported from social media or messaging apps are not accepted.
  • File size: Maximum 20 MB per file. Images are automatically resized to 960px on the longer side for AI scoring. This does not affect the original file, which must be retained for verification if required.
  • Minimum dimensions: 2,000 pixels on the shorter side. Images below this threshold will be rejected at the competition stage.
  • Metadata: Original EXIF metadata must be intact — camera make and model, date and time taken, and exposure settings. Images with stripped, altered, or absent EXIF are flagged as Unverified and may be excluded from competition at the judges' discretion.
  • No upscaling: Submitted images must be at native capture resolution. Upscaled images will be disqualified upon RAW verification.
  • Post-processing: Standard adjustments are permitted. AI generation, heavy compositing, and material scene alteration are prohibited. See Section 10 for full details.
Mobile photographers: Upload directly from your phone gallery. The platform accepts up to 20 MB and resizes automatically. Do not send your image through WhatsApp, Instagram, or any messaging app before uploading — this strips EXIF data.

05a Image Deletion & Contest Withdrawal

Registered users may permanently delete any image they have uploaded to Lens League Apex at any time via their dashboard or the image detail page. Deletion is irreversible — the image, its DDI score, tier classification, archetype, and all associated analysis data are removed permanently from the platform and cannot be recovered.

Effect on Scores and POTY Standing

Deleting a scored image will affect your DDI average, tier standing, and POTY qualification calculation. Your POTY standing is derived from your best 6 DDI scores per genre — if the deleted image was among your top 6 in any genre, your average for that genre will decrease and your qualification standing will be recalculated accordingly. Lens League Apex accepts no liability for any loss of standing, qualification, or prize eligibility resulting from a user-initiated image deletion.

Effect on Contest Entries

If you delete an image that has been submitted to an active contest — including the Weekly Challenge, any brand-sponsored contest, or the Open Competition — the image will be automatically withdrawn from that contest. The following rules apply:

  • Withdrawal takes effect immediately upon deletion. No resubstitution is permitted — you cannot replace a withdrawn entry with another image.
  • If the contest has a closing deadline and that deadline has passed, the withdrawal is still processed but your historical standing for that contest period will reflect the withdrawal.
  • For brand-sponsored contests: the same rules apply regardless of the sponsor. Deletion during an active sponsored contest window constitutes voluntary withdrawal and the sponsor will be notified where required by the sponsorship agreement.
  • For the Open Competition: if you have paid a ₹ 50 entry fee for the deleted image, no refund will be issued. Paid entries are non-refundable once submitted.
  • For POTY qualification: images that have contributed to your POTY standing in a completed platform year cannot be retroactively deleted from competition records. They may be deleted from your portfolio but the competition record will be retained for integrity purposes.

Platform Year Deadline — 31 December

The Lens League Apex platform year closes on 31 December. Any image deleted before 23:59 UTC on 31 December will be excluded from all year-end calculations including POTY standings, genre rankings, and prize eligibility for that platform year. Deletions after this deadline will not affect the final standings of the closed year.

Users are strongly advised to review their image portfolio carefully before deleting any scored images, particularly those that contribute to active contest entries or POTY qualification.

06 Competitions, POTY, BOW, Open Competition & Prizes

Monthly Competitions

  • Determined by DDI → Blind Peer Rating → Jury override (where triggered).
  • Separate Camera and Mobile Leagues. Genre-based within each League.
  • All scored images uploaded within the calendar month are automatically entered.
  • No restriction on when the image was originally captured.

Photographer of the Year (POTY)

  • Annual, per genre, across Camera and Mobile Leagues independently.
  • Eligibility: minimum 24 qualifying images in that genre during the subscription year.
  • POTY score: average of the photographer's top 6 DDI scores in that genre for the year.
  • No restriction on when the entered images were originally captured.

Grand POTY — Cumulative Prize Structure

The Grand POTY prize is awarded in addition to all category prizes the photographer has already won in that League. The Grand POTY prize does not replace or subsume any category prize. All prizes are cumulative. For example: a Grand POTY Camera winner who also won Gold in two genre categories receives the Grand POTY prize (₹ 10,00,000) plus both category Gold prizes (₹ 1,50,000 each) — a combined total of ₹ 13,00,000.

Body of Work (BOW)

  • Annual, cross-genre, no League split. Open to all active subscribers aged 18 or over.
  • Submission: 6–12 images as a unified thematic series. No restriction on original capture date.
  • Judged by Jury Legends only — no peer rating layer.
  • Prize pool: ₹ 5,25,000 — Gold ₹ 3,00,000 · Silver ₹ 1,50,000 · Bronze ₹ 75,000.
  • A photographer may win both a POTY prize and a BOW prize in the same year. Prizes are cumulative.

Open Competition

  • Annual, paid entry. ₹ 50 per image per genre. No League split.
  • 8 genre categories. One winner per genre. No Silver or Bronze.
  • Grand Open Winner receives ₹ 2,00,000 in addition to their category prize (₹ 75,000) — combined ₹ 2,75,000. All prizes cumulative.
  • Total Open prize pool: ₹ 8,00,000.
  • No restriction on when the entered image was originally captured.

General

  • Prize eligibility requires a verified account in good standing, age 18 or over, with no active policy violations.
  • All top 3 finalists in every category are subject to RAW verification before prizes are confirmed (see Section 08).
  • All prize decisions are final. Full rules are in the Contest Rules & Legal Framework.
  • Total annual prize pool: ₹ 63,25,000 — POTY ₹ 50,00,000 + Open ₹ 8,00,000 + BOW ₹ 5,25,000.

08 RAW Verification

The top 3 finalists in every category across all competitions are required to submit their original RAW or full-resolution source file to sreeks@gmail.com within 7 calendar days of notification. This applies to Monthly Contests, POTY, BOW, and Open Competition.

Verification checks EXIF consistency, native resolution (minimum 2,000px short side), and absence of AI generation or material compositing. Failure to submit, or failure of the verification check, results in immediate disqualification. The prize passes to the next placed entry. The verification decision is final.

Random spot checks: In addition to finalist verification, Lens League Apex may request RAW file submission for any image at any time, regardless of score or competition status. This is a platform integrity measure applied at our discretion. Failure to comply within 7 calendar days will result in image removal and account review under the fraud policy in Section 10.

Platform processing note: Images uploaded to Lens League Apex are resized to 960px on the longer side for AI scoring. This is the scoring copy only. Always retain your original full-resolution file — it is required for verification if you are a finalist or selected for a random spot check.

10 Image Authenticity & AI-Generated Content

Lens League Apex is a platform for genuine photography — images captured by a camera or mobile device by the submitting photographer. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • AI-generated images (Midjourney, DALL-E, Gemini, Stable Diffusion, Firefly, or any generative AI tool)
  • Images primarily created through generative AI compositing
  • Heavy manipulation presented as unmanipulated photography
  • Sky replacement, object addition or removal that materially alters the captured scene
  • Images taken by another photographer

Permitted: exposure adjustment, colour grading, cropping, sharpening, noise reduction, dodging and burning, and focus stacking from multiple frames of the same subject captured by the photographer.

Perceptual hash detection, EXIF analysis, AI detection, and RAW verification are used to identify violations. Violations at any stage — including after a prize is awarded — result in the following immediate and permanent consequences:

  • All images permanently removed from the platform
  • Account permanently deleted — the photographer is banned from registering any future account
  • All prizes forfeited — including prizes already disbursed, which must be returned upon demand
  • Permanent ban from all current and future Lens League Apex competitions
