01 Scoring Methodology

Lens League Apex uses the Apex DDI Engine, a proprietary AI-powered scoring system that evaluates photographs across five modules: Degree of Difficulty (DoD), Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment (DM), Wonder Factor, and Affective Quotient (AQ).

Each module is weighted according to the genre submitted by the photographer. Final scores are calculated by a weighted formula and subject to Apex Layer rules including Soul Bonus, Humanity Check, Iconic Wall, and Plateau Penalty adjustments.

Scores are indicative, objective assessments of photographic merit based on the submitted genre and image content. The specific formula weights and scoring parameters are proprietary and not disclosed in full to protect intellectual property.

Important: Scores reflect the engine's assessment at the time of submission. Calibration updates may affect how future images are scored but will not retroactively change existing scores.

02 AI Limitations Disclaimer

The Apex DDI Engine is an AI-powered system and is subject to the inherent limitations of artificial intelligence, including but not limited to:

  • Occasional misidentification of subject matter, genre, or technique
  • Scoring variations between identical or near-identical submissions due to model non-determinism
  • Potential bias in favour of or against certain photographic styles not well-represented in training data
  • Inability to account for contextual information not visible in the image itself

Lens League Apex makes no warranty that AI scores are infallible, complete, or free from error. Scores should be treated as one data point in a photographer's development, not as a definitive or final judgment of artistic merit.

03 Peer, Jury & Judge Evaluation

Certain images — particularly those in the Creative genre scoring 7.0 or above, or those flagged by the engine for exceptional technical complexity — are automatically referred for human judge review.

Judge-referred images will be evaluated by qualified photography judges appointed by Lens League Apex. Judge decisions are final and binding on competition results. Human judge scores may override AI scores for competition ranking purposes.

Peer rating and jury evaluation features may be introduced in future platform updates. The introduction of peer or jury rating will be communicated to members in advance and governed by updated terms.

07 Image Authenticity & AI-Generated Content

Lens League Apex is a platform for genuine photography — images captured by a camera or mobile device by the submitting photographer. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • AI-generated images (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, or any AI image generator)
  • Images primarily created through generative AI compositing
  • Heavily manipulated images presented as unmanipulated photographs
  • Images taken by another photographer submitted under your account

The engine employs perceptual hash duplicate detection and EXIF metadata analysis to identify suspicious submissions. Images flagged as AI-generated or duplicate may be removed and the account suspended without notice.

Genuine photographic manipulation including exposure adjustment, colour grading, cropping, focus stacking, and HDR merging of frames taken by the submitting photographer is permitted.

08 EXIF Metadata & Camera Verification

Lens League Apex extracts and analyses EXIF metadata from uploaded images to verify camera authenticity. Images with intact EXIF data from a recognised camera manufacturer are marked as Verified.

Absence of EXIF metadata does not automatically disqualify an image but will result in an Unverified status. Suspicious or inconsistent metadata patterns may trigger manual review.

For best results and highest scores, upload original JPEG files directly from your camera without stripping metadata. Camera brand and model do not influence scoring — all manufacturers are treated equally.
