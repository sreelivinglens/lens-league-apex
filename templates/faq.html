Frequently Asked Questions

Lens League Apex  ·  Everything you need to know

01 Scoring & the DDI Engine

What is the APEX DDI Engine?
DDI stands for Depth, Disruption, and Intent — the three core dimensions the engine evaluates. It analyses your photograph across five modules and produces a score from 0 to 100. Unlike human judging, the score is consistent, bias-free, and based on photographic craft rather than personal taste.
What do the tiers mean?
Your score maps to a tier that describes the level of craft in the image: Apprentice (developing), Practitioner (solid craft), Master (exceptional), Grandmaster (rare, requires RAW verification). Tiers are shown publicly. Raw scores are visible only to you.
Why can't I see the exact score breakdown?
The individual module scores (the five DDI dimensions) are not shown publicly to protect the integrity of the scoring system and prevent photographers from optimising for the algorithm rather than making better photographs. Your overall score and tier are always visible.
How long does scoring take?
Most images score within 30–60 seconds. Complex images or peak usage periods can take up to 2 minutes. Keep the tab open during scoring. If scoring fails, you can retry from your dashboard.
Does editing or post-processing affect my score?
Yes — the engine evaluates the final image as submitted. Thoughtful editing that enhances the photograph's intent scores well. Heavy-handed or inconsistent processing scores lower. The engine rewards craft at every stage, including post-processing.

02 Plans & Subscription

What's included in the free plan?
Free members get 4 scored images per month and 1 weekly challenge submission per week. You can view the leaderboard and browse the platform freely. Competition entry (POTY, BOW, Open) requires a subscription.
What does the Member plan include?
At Rs.199/month, Members get unlimited image scoring, 3 weekly challenge submissions per week, leaderboard ranking, and entry to all competitions (POTY, Body of Work, Open).
How do I cancel my subscription?
Log in, go to your Dashboard, and click Cancel subscription in the subscription panel. No email needed. Access continues until the end of your current billing period. See our Refund Policy for details.
What is Camera League vs Mobile League?
Camera League is for images taken on dedicated cameras (DSLR, mirrorless, film). Mobile League is for images taken on smartphones. You declare your league when subscribing. The EXIF metadata in your images is used to verify this. Competing in the wrong league results in a warning and, after three mismatches, a suspension of contest access.

03 Weekly Challenge

How does the weekly challenge work?
Every Monday a new prompt is announced. You have until Sunday 23:59 UTC to submit. Free members can submit 1 image, subscribers can submit 3. Winners are announced the following Monday evening (7pm IST) by DDI score. The gallery is blind while the challenge is open — no scores or rankings are shown until results are published.
Can I submit more than my weekly limit?
Yes — you can buy extra submissions at Rs.49 per image. Free members can submit up to 4 total, subscribers up to 6 total per week. Click "Buy more images" on the challenge page when your base submissions are used.
How are winners selected?
The top 3 submissions by DDI score are automatically selected as winners when the challenge closes. The admin reviews these before the announcement and may adjust if an image is off-topic or violates the brief. DDI score is the primary criterion.
Do weekly challenge scores count toward POTY?
Yes — any image submitted to the weekly challenge is scored by the DDI engine. That score counts toward your Photographer of the Year standing in its genre. This is the most direct way to build your POTY score week by week.

04 Competitions

What is Photographer of the Year (POTY)?
POTY is Lens League Apex's annual competition. Your top 6 DDI scores in each genre are averaged to produce your POTY standing. A minimum of 24 scored images in a genre is required to qualify for prizes. Entry fee is Rs.199 per genre.
What is Body of Work (BOW)?
BOW is an annual series competition. You submit a cohesive set of images with a thematic statement. Entry fee is Rs.299. One submission per platform year.
What is the Open Competition?
The Open is a pay-per-image competition with no subscription required. Entry fee is Rs.39 per image. Any registered user can enter.

05 Images & Copyright

Do I keep copyright over my images?
Yes. You retain full copyright. By uploading, you grant Lens League Apex a limited licence to display your images on the platform and in promotional materials referencing the platform (e.g. showcasing winning entries on social media). We do not sell or license your images to third parties.
What happens if my image is flagged as AI-generated?
The platform checks for AI-generated images as part of the upload process. If your image is flagged, you can appeal by emailing sreeks@gmail.com with subject Appeal — [username] — [image ID] and attaching your original RAW file within 7 days. All appeals are reviewed.
Can I delete my images?
Yes — you can delete any image from your dashboard or from the image detail page at any time. Deleting an image is permanent and cannot be undone. Your DDI scores, tier ratings and POTY standing will be recalculated to reflect the deletion.
What happens to my contest entries if I delete an image?
If you delete an image that has been submitted to the Weekly Challenge, a sponsored contest, or the Open Competition, it will be automatically withdrawn from that competition. If the contest deadline has already passed on 31 December of the platform year, the image will still be removed but your historical contest standing for that period will reflect the withdrawal. We strongly recommend not deleting images that are part of active contest entries.
Will deleting an image affect my POTY qualification?
Yes. Your POTY standing is calculated from your best 6 DDI scores per genre. Deleting a scored image removes it from this calculation. If it was one of your top 6 in a genre, your average will drop and your qualification standing will change accordingly. Think carefully before deleting scored images — especially high-scoring ones.
Can I delete an image after it has been scored?
Yes. There is no restriction on deleting scored images. However, once deleted the score is gone permanently — you cannot recover it. The DDI analysis, archetype, and improvement tip associated with that image will also be removed.
