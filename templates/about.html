Why We Exist

The first photograph was taken in 1826.

In the 200 years since, photography has become the most widely practised creative skill on earth. Chefs have Michelin stars. Tennis players have ATP rankings. Cricketers have ICC ratings. Wine has Robert Parker. Chess has Elo.

Photographers have likes.

Likes from family. Encouragement from friends. Polite silence from strangers. And if you are lucky, a club judge who says 'lovely bokeh' before moving on to the next image without explaining what was actually wrong.

Nobody tells you the truth about your photography. Not because people do not care. Because photography is personal. Criticism of the image feels like criticism of the person. And so the honest feedback never comes. Photographers stay stuck. They improve slowly, invisibly, with no way to measure how far they have come or how far they have to go.

Shutter League was built to fix that.

We are a photography skill assessment platform. Every photograph uploaded to Shutter League is evaluated across five dimensions — Depth of Detail, Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment, Wonder Factor, and Affective Quotient — by an AI engine trained on photographic principles. The score is specific, the feedback is actionable, and unlike a club judge, the engine has no ego, no favourites, and no social relationship to protect.

For the first time in 200 years of photography, photographers have a rating.

We are based in Bengaluru, India. Built by photographers, for photographers. Every serious photographer deserves an honest answer to the question they have always wanted to ask: how good is my work, really?

What We Offer

Digital services for serious photographers

AI-Powered Image Scoring (DDI)

Every uploaded photograph is scored across five dimensions: Depth of Detail, Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment, Wonder Factor, and Affective Quotient. Scores are on a 0–10 scale per dimension.

AI Mentor Feedback

Every scored image receives specific, dimension-level feedback and improvement paths. Available on all paid tiers.

Score History & Progress Tracking

Track your improvement arc across uploads, genres, and dimensions over time.

Weekly Practice Submissions

A themed weekly prompt open to all members. DDI-scored, no jury. Positioned as structured practice, not formal evaluation.

Photographer of the Year (POTY)

A year-long scoring programme tracking each member's top-6 scores per genre. Results evaluated by a human jury at year end. Eligibility requires a Camera or Mobile subscription.

Juried Photography Programmes

Periodic open submission programmes evaluated by a panel of photography professionals. Human jury required for all formal evaluations.

Peer Rating

Subscribed members can participate in blind peer evaluation of each other's work.

Subscriptions

Monthly plans (Mobile League at ₹99/month, Camera League at ₹199/month) providing platform access, scoring, feedback, and programme eligibility.

Our Commitment

Transparency in scoring.
Integrity in evaluation.

We are committed to transparency in scoring, integrity in evaluation, and privacy in how we handle member data. Every score is generated by the same engine, applied consistently across all images, with no human subjectivity in the initial assessment.

Where human jury evaluation applies — in formal annual programmes — it is disclosed explicitly.

We are building the infrastructure that photography has lacked for 200 years. We intend to do it honestly.
