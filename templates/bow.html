Body of Work

The platform's
most prestigious competition.

Not a collection of your best images. A unified thematic series — with a single narrative arc, a coherent visual language, and a point of view that holds together from first frame to last. Judged exclusively by Jury Legends. No engine. No peer rating. Pure human judgement.

Pre-qualifying Requirement
You must hold a rolling average of 7.5 or above across your last 20 scored images to unlock the Body of Work entry form. This ensures the jury reviews only work that has proven itself at the highest platform standard. Subscribers only. Active subscription must be maintained through submission close.
Submission Requirements
  • 1
    Submit between 6 and 12 images. More is not better — the jury evaluates coherence and editorial restraint. Every image must earn its place in the series.
  • 2
    The series must be cross-genre. Images from a single genre are not accepted. The BOW is designed to demonstrate range within a unified vision.
  • 3
    You must submit a series statement of 80 to 200 words. This is read by every juror before they view a single image. It frames your intent — not your technique.
  • 4
    All images must have been previously scored on the platform with a minimum individual score of 6.5. You cannot submit work that has not gone through the Apex DDI Engine.
  • 5
    Image order matters. You will sequence your submission — the jury views images in the exact order you specify. Sequence is part of the judging criteria.
  • 6
    One submission per photographer per year. You may not withdraw and resubmit once the submission window closes. Review your series carefully before submitting.
Prize Structure
Award What it recognises Prize
◆  Gold The single most powerful unified body of work submitted that year ₹3,00,000
◆  Silver Outstanding series with exceptional coherence and narrative strength ₹1,50,000
◆  Bronze Remarkable submission demonstrating distinctive visual voice ₹75,000

The jury may decline to award Gold if no submission meets the required standard. Highly commended citations may be awarded at the jury's discretion, carrying no monetary prize.
