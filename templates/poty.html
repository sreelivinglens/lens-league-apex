The 6-6-12 Rule — Your Professional Credibility Gate

Shutter League uses a residency-based ranking system — the same logic as ICC cricket rankings. A single brilliant photograph does not make a rank. Consistent performance over time does.

Number
Rule
Gate
6 Months
You must hold an active Camera or Mobile subscription for 6 cumulative months. The clock runs only while subscribed — it pauses if you cancel and resumes from where it stopped when you resubscribe.
6 Images
You must have at least 6 DDI-scored images in your qualifying genre. Six images is the statistical minimum for a meaningful rank — fewer and the data is not reliable enough to place you on the official leaderboard.
12 Pool
Your rank is calculated from your best or most recent 12 images. As you upload more, older images rotate out. Your rank reflects your current form, not a single peak performance years ago.

How Rankings Are Determined

Every image uploaded to Shutter League is evaluated by the Apex DDI Engine — a five-dimension scoring framework covering Depth of Detail, Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment, Wonder Factor, and Aesthetic Quality. Scores are generated automatically and consistently, with no human subjectivity in the initial assessment.

A photographer's annual ranking is calculated from their best six DDI scores in their strongest genre during the calendar year. You qualify in one genre — the one where your best six scores average highest. The top-20 photographers per genre proceed to jury review, where a panel of photography professionals evaluates the work and confirms the final rankings.

This two-stage process — AI consistency followed by human judgement — is the same standard used by the world's leading photography award programmes.
