The 6-6-12 Rule — Why Your Rank Means Something

A single brilliant photograph doesn't make a rank. Neither does a single great month. Shutter League uses a residency-based system — the same logic as ICC cricket rankings and ATP tennis points. You have to show up consistently, across time, before the ranking is yours. And because the pool rolls — old images drop off as new ones arrive — your rank reflects what you've shot recently, not a peak performance from three years ago.

How Rankings Are Determined
The scoring and jury process ▾

Most photography awards are judged by a panel of humans who see your image once, in a batch, against thousands of others. Shutter League works differently. The Apex DDI Engine evaluates every image individually, immediately, across five dimensions — Depth of Detail, Visual Disruption, Decisive Moment, Wonder Factor, and Aesthetic Quality. The score is generated the same way for every photographer, every time, with no human subjectivity in the initial assessment.

Your annual POTY ranking is calculated from your best six DDI scores in your strongest genre. You compete in one genre — the one where your scores are highest. A wildlife photographer qualifies on wildlife alone. The top-20 photographers per genre then proceed to jury review, where photography professionals verify the work and confirm the final rankings.

AI consistency first. Human judgement second. This two-stage process means your rank is both scientifically defensible and professionally validated — not a matter of who a judge happened to notice on a given day.
